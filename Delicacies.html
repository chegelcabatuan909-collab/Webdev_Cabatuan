Pinato is a traditional Filipino delicacy made from roasted peanuts coated in caramelized sugar. Crunchy and sweet, it is a favorite snack for both young and old. Usually shaped into small bite-sized clusters or bars, pinato is often sold in local stores, markets, and pasalubong centers. Its simple yet delightful flavor makes it a perfect companion for merienda or travel snacks, giving a true taste of Filipino ingenuity and love for sweets.

Beef and Chicken Satti

Grilled skewers of marinated meat paired with sticky rice cubes and served with a sweet and spicy sauce. Often enjoyed for breakfast.

Chavacano Paella

A local version of paella where rice is colored with turmeric and cooked with seafood, chicken, chorizo, and vegetables. It reflects both Spanish and Filipino influences.

Lokot-Lokot (Lukot-Lukot)

A crispy Tausug delicacy made from glutinous rice flour. The batter is drizzled into hot oil to form crunchy mats that are folded into pieces.
